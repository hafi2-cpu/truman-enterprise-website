Who We Are

A Volunteer-Run Charity Opening the Canal to All

We're a volunteer-run charity who has been offering canal trips to people across the West Midlands and beyond for over 50 years. We provide day trips for community and family groups, especially those with elderly, disabled, or special needs members, as well as individuals facing health challenges.

Our narrowboat Enterprise starts from Hatherton Marina and cruises the beautiful Staffs & Worcs Canal to Coven, Autherley Junction or Penkridge — each route a unique experience. A peaceful day on the water brings relaxation, connection, and enjoyment to all aboard.

Narrowboat Enterprise

Nearly 50 Years of Serving Our Community

Originally built in 1978 by Cammell Laird apprentices, Enterprise has been specially adapted for passengers with mobility needs. She carries up to 12 passengers with ramp access, a hydraulic lift, large picture windows, central heating, and an accessible toilet — everything needed for a comfortable and safe day on the canal.

Built in 1978 as Symphony by Cammell Laird apprentices for the Peter Le Marchant Trust, incorporating materials from naval destroyers. Refurbished and renamed Walsall Enterprise in 1981 to offer outings for Walsall residents, then transferred to the HF Truman Trust in 2005. Renamed simply Enterprise in 2020, upgrades over the decades include new engines, lifts and facilities.

In 1965, dedicated youth leaders in Walsall raised funds for a narrowboat, leading to the launch of HF Truman in 1968. The group became the HF Truman Narrowboat Trust charity in 1992, moving to Hatherton Marina in 1981. Renamed Truman Enterprise Narrowboat Trust in 2006 after acquiring the 70ft Enterprise from Walsall MBC Social Services.

Our volunteers are passionate about the inland waterways of the West Midlands. We want to promote the benefits of a great day out on the canal to groups who might otherwise find it difficult to access the countryside. We care about our passengers and will try to accommodate everyone's needs.
