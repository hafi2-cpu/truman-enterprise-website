More Information

What to Expect

Narrowboat Enterprise is a 70ft canal boat designed especially for carrying passengers with mobility issues. It has:

  • Ramps & hydraulic passenger lift
  • Seating for up to 12 passengers (minimum 2 leaders/staff required)
  • Large open front deck
  • Small kitchen with fridge & 2 gas burners
  • Large open cabin with deep picture windows
  • Central heating
  • Accessible toilet
  • Free hot & cold drinks for all passengers
  • Qualified skipper and crew member on every trip
  • Free secure parking at the Marina

Crew Competency

Each trip has a skipper and crew member who have passed the appropriate course accredited by the National Community Boats Association (NCBA). All Trust skippers hold the Certificate in Community Boat Management (CCBM). Crew members hold a Community Crew Course (CCC) certificate. All crew undertake update training annually.

DBS Checks

The designated skipper and crew on every trip are required to undergo an enhanced DBS check.

Risk Assessments

The Trust undertakes risk assessments which are regularly reviewed. Risk assessments are available on request.

Insurance

The Trust carries Public and Employer Liability Insurance of £10,000,000 in each instance. Our current insurance certificate is on display on board the Enterprise.

Payment may be made by cheque or by BACS — details will be shown on your invoice.

For BACS payments, please refer to your invoice for our bank details and quote your invoice number or trip date to ensure your payment is allocated correctly.

Payment is due 28 days before each trip. Receipts are available on request.
